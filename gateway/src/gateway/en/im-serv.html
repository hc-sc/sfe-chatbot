Branch IM services

Open government coordination

Open Government is about making government more accessible to everyone. This means giving greater access to government data and information to the Canadian public and the businesses community. The coordination service includes:

  • Maintenance of the departmental dataset inventory,
  • Provision of templates, advice and guidance in support of the Open Data and Open Information directive,
  • Facilitation for the publishing of datasets and information resources on the TBS Open Government Portal on behalf of OPI branches.

Requesting the service

Please include the details of your request within an email and send to: IM Info / Info GI (HC/SC)

IM policy advice and guidance

There are many policy instruments that guide and direct how information must be managed and our responsibilities to support these. Services include:

  • Interpretation of IM policy instruments (internal corporate and external [for example: LAC and TBS])
  • Provision of feedback for Branch created IM policy instruments (processes/procedures/guidelines)

Requesting the service

Please include the details of your request within an email and send to: IM Info / Info GI (HC/SC)

Functional classification standard advice and guidance

The Functional Classification Standard (FCS) is the organization of information resources based on HC / PHAC's business functions and activities, which are determined from the legislation requirements, internal policies, mandate and mission.

Visit the FCS GCpedia page GCpedia page for more information

Services include:

  • Provision of advice and guidance on the FCS

    To request this service, please include the details of your request within an email and send to: IM Info / Info GI (HC/SC)

  • Maintenance of the FCS via change management requests

    To request this service, please discuss the details of the requirement with your Information Management (IM) Advisor who will work with IKMD to facilitate the changes.

IM learning and knowledge services

The Learning and Knowledge Services (LKS) team in the Information and Knowledge Management Division (IKMD) is dedicated to providing Information Management (IM) learning opportunities to help all Health Canada (HC) and the Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC) employees, managers, and IM specialists acquire the knowledge and skills necessary to fulfill their roles and responsibilities in managing information resources effectively.

Services include:

  • IM training (offered via classroom [NCR], WebEx and self-paced online courses) covering:
    • IM best practices
    • tools
    • standards
    • guidelines, and
    • advice

    Consult the LKS-GCpedia page to see what sessions are available, along with the registration information.

  • Dedicated training sessions for groups of 10 or more employees can be arranged.

    To schedule an on-site (NCR) or WebEx dedicated session, contact the IM Info / Info GI (HC/SC).

  • Coordination of IM related activities, events and communications to build IM awareness throughout HC/PHAC, while working closely with the IM community.

    For more information, contact the IM Info / Info GI (HC/SC).

IM systems administration and management & client support functions

The IM Tools unit within the Information and Knowledge Management Division (IKMD) provides client service support and guidance for specific tools including LRS, GCdocs and RDIMS/EDRMS.

Services include:

  • Providing litigation Support using e-Discovery tool

    To request this service, contact your Information Management (IM) Advisor

  • Providing reports on IM tools and applications

    To request this service, please include the details of your request within an email and send to: IM Info / Info GI (HC/SC)

For any other questions related to IM tools and applications, please email IM Info / Info GI (HC/SC).

IM client services

The Information Knowledge and Management Division (IKMD) within the Information Management Services Directorate (IMSD) provides IM services to clients that include:

  • file plan development (electronic and paper)
  • on-site and off-site storage management and inventory
  • analyzing IM requirements for clients on an ad hoc basis
  • providing IM advice and recommendations to clients
  • Records Retention and Disposition Schedules (RRDS) development and maintenance (for electronic and paper files)
  • litigation support for Access to information and privacy (ATIP) clients
  • paper file creation, and
  • disposition services (paper and electronic)

To request any of the listed services, please include the details of your request within an email and send to: IM Info / Info GI (HC/SC)

Health library: research tools and services

The Health Library provides services and e-resources that support the policy, research and regulatory activities of Health Canada and the Public Health Agency of Canada.

Research tools and services include:

  • Reference and literature searches – provides you with in-depth database and citation searches
  • Update notifications – receive automatic alerts to stay current on your areas of interest
  • Training and information sessions – learn how to access the information you need from your desktop
  • Document delivery/ Interlibrary loans – order articles, books or documents at no cost
  • Publication metrics and impact analysis – assistance with assessing publication uptake, h-indices and journal impact factors
  • Citation management – online citation software to help organize, store and share citations, as well as format bibliographies and documents.
    • RefWorks: All Health Canada and Public Health Agency of Canada employees have access to RefWorks at no cost. With RefWorks, you can format your bibliography in APA, MLA, AMA and over 200 other styles.
    • Publication support: Questions about formatting? Wondering if it is a reputable or a predatory publisher?
