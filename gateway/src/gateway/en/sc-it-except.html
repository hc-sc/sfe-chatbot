Exceptions to standard IT practices

The IT policies implemented at HC / PHAC ensure the IT infrastructure is well managed, secure and protects physical and information assets.

Sometimes exceptions to these policies are necessary to support business needs. Some examples of exception requests may include:

  • Disabling the nightly shutdown process

    You may require the disabling of the nightly shutdown process to ensure their computer(s) can execute computing tasks outside of core working hours or overnight.

  • Disabling the screensaver policy

    You may require uninterrupted display of presentations or other visual content.

  • Installing CD burning software

    Based on business requirements, you may require the ability to write to CD/DVD media.

  • Unblocking specialized business devices

    Based on business requirements, you may require enablement of a specialized business device, such as a digital camera, voice recorder, or local printer, etc.

  • Enabling HC / PHAC computers to read unencrypted USB sticks

    You may receive unencrypted USB sticks from external partners that contain business information. To access the information, IT Security can enable your device to read the unencrypted USB stick.

  • Request access to a blocked website

    Based on business requirements, you can submit a request to access blocked websites.

Requests for exceptions to standard IT practices undergo thorough reviews by IT Security, in consultation with the requestors and the program areas' executive management. Exceptions are granted only when business needs would be hindered or impeded if the change to the default security settings or configuration on a computing device does not occur. Approval of an exception request is often accompanied by a set of conditions, an acceptance of related risk and defined boundaries that are specific to the exception.

Request an exception

When requesting an exception to a standard IT practice, you will want to:

  • identify and describe the desired exception
  • provide a business rationale that will explain why the exception is necessary and what alternatives have been considered

An IT Security representative will respond to all requests, facilitate the review and consultation process and guide the request through to its completion.
