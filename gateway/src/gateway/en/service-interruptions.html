Shared Service Canada's schedule of planned IT service interruptions

Shared Services Canada (SSC) is one of HC’s and PHAC’s IT service providers and it is their responsibility, on behalf of all its client departments, to maintain the Government of Canada network and IT infrastructure and ensure that the network and its components remain healthy and secure. To do so, regular maintenance activities must occur, such as; security updates and patches, software upgrades, infrastructure and hardware upgrades and replacements.

SSC manages and coordinates a schedule for upcoming, planned IT service interruptions. Whenever possible, this information is shared in advance with Information Management Service Directorate (IMSD), to help us proactively communicate, to our HC and PHAC clients, the dates and times of planned interruptions for IT services. Our goal is to share this information with our clients to increase transparency and to allow our clients to be aware of interruptions that may impact them.

Whenever possible, regular maintenance activities are performed within the SSC IT Maintenance Window. The regular SSC IT Maintenance Window occurs outside business hours, on Sunday 12:00 am to 12:00 pm, every week.

  • Planned and unplanned IT service interruptions

    Planned IT service interruptions

    Periods of time, when IM/IT services may be unavailable due to required maintenance activities. These maintenance activities are performed to prevent unplanned and emergency IT service interruptions (unplanned or emergency IT Service Interruptions will not appear on the calendar) from occurring and preventing significant IM/IT service ‘downtime’ or unavailability for clients.

    Significant planning and cost is involved in supporting and executing on planned service interruptions, as external vendors are often involved.

    Unplanned IT Service Interruptions (may also be referred to as Emergency IT Service Interruptions)

    Disruptions in IT services that occur;

    • Without any advance notice: Sometimes, issues or circumstances occur when an IT service or services are unavailable, but are expected to be fully functioning. When this occurs, an investigation is immediately launched and actions toward resolution are taken to ensure that the IT service(s) can be safely reinstated and service availability to clients resumes as quickly as possible; or
    • With little advance notice: There are times when circumstances warrant or information is received that identifies that urgent action must be taken to resolve an imminent or immediate issue or to prevent an issue from occurring on, or related to, the GC network. When this type of event occurs and urgent action is required, SSC informs IMSD, but with very little advance notice before action must be taken.
    • In such circumstances, it is believed that the urgent action required to mitigate the imminent risk or issue to the GC network and its components are greater than the potential, temporary disruption to business.

  • What IT Services can clients and users expect to be available during the SSC regular maintenance window?
    • The maintenance window is a period of time that is dedicated to IT maintenance and therefore, clients and users may reasonably expect that IT services may be unavailable during this part, or all, of this period of time.
    • The expected ‘downtime’ or ‘unavailability’ of services during the timeframe identified for a planned IT service interruption is an estimate.
    • Planned IT service interruptions and maintenance activities carried out during the maintenance window are planned; however, if things do not go as anticipated; the timeframe to complete the activities may extend within the maintenance window and, on occasion, outside of the window, under difficult circumstances.

Disclaimer

HC and PHAC do not have a 24/7 IT Service Support Model and therefore, do not offer support services outside of the defined timeframe, except in exceptional circumstances (e.g. emergencies related to the health and safety of Canadians). HC and PHAC receive IT service and support, through the National Service Desk, between the hours of 7:00am to 8:00pm (EST), Monday through Friday, except statutory holidays. Outside of these operating hours, IT support is of a ‘best effort’ basis (i.e. if services or applications go down outside of operational hours, resolution may not occur until operational hours resume).

For immediate or urgent assistance, please call the National Service Desk at 613-954-8718 (NCR) or 1-800-416-0358 (regions)
