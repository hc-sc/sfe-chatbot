Choosing the right PSD

To determine which PSD is appropriate for your business needs, consider:

What is your business requirement to procure a PSD?

Determine why you need a PSD and what business requirement you will use it for. Consider alternative solutions for information sharing within HC / PHAC, such as the using the current records and document management systems (e.g. shared drives, RDIMS, GDdocs), if the information is Protected B or a lower sensitivity.

What types of business information will you be temporarily storing on a PSD?

Business documents, such as Word documents, PDF files, Excel spreadsheets and PowerPoint presentations are generally small in size and can usually be accommodated by a USB stick available in 8GB, 16GB, or 32GB capacities. Larger files, such as HD video, SAS data sets, virtual machines (VMs), or AutoCAD drawings may require more substantial storage space that may be better serviced with the use of an external hard drive (available in 1TB, 2TB, 4TB, 6TB, 8TB, and 10TB capacities).

What type of equipment will your PSD connect to?

The type of PSD you select may depend on what type of equipment it will be connected to. Some PSDs are only compatible with HC / PHAC computers. Others PSDs may be used with non-standard GC hardware such as printers, scientific equipment, or other devices that store data to be transferred to a corporate repository, but do not have a graphical user interface or traditional operating system such as Microsoft Windows.

What sensitivity of information will your PSD store temporarily?

Determine the sensitivity level of the information you intend to store on your PSD. You will be asked to specify the sensitivity level of the information that your PSD will store during the PSD request process. Please refer to the Protection and Security Categorization of Information for information sensitivity definitions and examples to assist you in categorizing your information into the appropriate sensitivity level(s).
